Solar longitude, often written Ls, is a kind of calendar to measure the time of year for solar system bodies other than the Earth. Since the [Gregorian Calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gregorian_calendar) is based on Earth's 365.25 day orbital period, it isn't as useful for other planetary bodies that may have much shorter or longer seasons. Instead, solar longitude measures the time of year as an angle measured from vernal (spring) equinox in the northern hemisphere. One month on Earth corresponds to a solar longitude interval of approximately 30°, and each season starts at a multiple of 90°.
